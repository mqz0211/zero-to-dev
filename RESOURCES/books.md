# Books

> Free/legally-available options are noted. Not every great book is
> free — some are worth buying or borrowing from a library.

## Foundations

- **"Automate the Boring Stuff with Python"** — Al Sweigart *(free to read online)* — the best first programming book for absolute beginners.
- **"Structured Computer Organization"** — Andrew Tanenbaum — how a computer actually works under the hood, once you're ready to go deeper.
- **"The Pragmatic Programmer"** — Hunt & Thomas — the most commonly recommended "how to think like a professional developer" book.

## Programming & Software Engineering

- **"Clean Code"** — Robert C. Martin — divisive but influential; read critically, not as gospel.
- **"Cracking the Coding Interview"** — Gayle Laakmann McDowell — internship/junior interview prep, algorithms + interview process.
- **"Designing Data-Intensive Applications"** — Martin Kleppmann — once you're intermediate+, the best systems-design book available.

## Cybersecurity

- **"The Web Application Hacker's Handbook"** — Stuttard & Pinto — a foundational, deep reference on web app security.
- **"Hacking: The Art of Exploitation"** — Jon Erickson — lower-level, includes real exploitation fundamentals.
- **"The Linux Command Line"** — William Shotts *(free to read online)* — pairs directly with [`FUNDAMENTALS/linux.md`](../FUNDAMENTALS/linux.md).

## AI / Machine Learning

- **"Dive into Deep Learning"** — Zhang, Lipton, Li, Smola *(free, interactive, code-included)* — genuinely excellent and free.
- **"Deep Learning"** — Goodfellow, Bengio, Courville *(free to read online)* — the field's standard reference text, dense but comprehensive.
- **"Hands-On Machine Learning with Scikit-Learn, Keras, and TensorFlow"** — Aurélien Géron — the most commonly recommended practical ML book.

## Data Science

- **"Python for Data Analysis"** — Wes McKinney (creator of pandas) — the practical standard reference.
- **"Storytelling with Data"** — Cole Nussbaumer Knaflic — how to actually communicate what your analysis found.

## Career / General

- **"So Good They Can't Ignore You"** — Cal Newport — a genuinely useful reframe on "follow your passion" career advice.
- **"The Missing README"** — Chris Riccomini & Dmitriy Ryaboy *(free to read online)* — what nobody tells new engineers about the actual job.
